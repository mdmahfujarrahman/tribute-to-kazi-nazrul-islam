Kazi Nazrul Islam

1899 - 1976

Bengali Poet, Writer, Musician

Islam, Kazi Nazrul (1899-1976) national poet of Bangladesh, called the 'rebel poet' for his brave resistance to all forms of repression. His poetry, with its vibrant rhythms and iconoclastic themes, forms a striking contrast to rabindranath tagore's poetry.

Known as the Rebel Poet.

The following list is a time line of Nazrul Life

  • 1899 - Nazrul was born on Wednesday in the village of Churulia, Asansol Sadar, Paschim Bardhaman district of the Bengal Presidency.
  • 1910 - Nazrul left the troupe and enrolled at the Searsole Raj High School in Raniganj.
  • 1914 - Nazrul studied in the Darirampur School (now Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University) in Trishal, Mymensingh District.
  • 1917 - Nazrul studied up to grade 10 but did not appear for the matriculation pre-test examination, he joined the British Indian Army at the age of eighteen.
  • 1919 - His first prose work, "Life of a Vagabond" ('Baunduler Atmakahini'), was published
  • 1920 - He published his first novel Bandhan-hara.
  • 1921 - Nazrul was engaged to Nargis, the niece of a well-known Muslim publisher, Ali Akbar Khan, in Daulatpur, Comilla.
  • 1923 - Nazrul was arrested on and charged with sedition.
  • 1924 - During his visit to Comilla in 1921, Nazrul met a young Bengali Hindu woman, Pramila Devi, with whom he fell in love, and they married.
  • 1926 - His wife and young son Bulbul, Nazrul settled at Grace Cottage, Krishnanagar in Krishnanagar. His work began to transform as he wrote poetry and songs that articulated the aspirations of the working class, a sphere of his work known as "mass music".
  • 1928 - Nazrul began working as a lyricist, composer, and music director for His Master's Voice Gramophone Company.
  • 1930 - His book Pralayshikha was banned and he faced charges of sedition by the British Indian. colonial government.
  • 1931 - He was sent to jail and released, after the Gandhiâ€“Irwin Pact was signed.
  • 1933 - Nazrul published a collection of essays titled "Modern World Literature", in which he analyses different styles and themes of literature.
  • 1939 - Nazrul began working for Calcutta Radio, supervising the production and broadcasting of the station's musical programs.
  • 1941 - Hearing about the death of Rabindranath Tagore, a shocked Nazrul composed two poems in Tagore's memory. One of the two poems, "Rabihara" (loss of Rabi, or without Rabi), was broadcast on the All India Radio.
  • 1942 - Within months, Nazrul himself fell ill and gradually began losing his power of speech. His behaviour became erratic, he started spending recklessly and fell into financial difficulties. In spite of her own illness, his wife constantly cared for her husband.Spending four months there without making progress, Nazrul and his family began living a quiet life in India.
  • 1952 - He was transferred to a psychiatric hospital in Ranchi.Through the efforts of a large group of admirers who called themselves the "Nazrul Treatment Society", Nazrul and Promila were sent to London, then to Vienna for treatment.The examining doctors said he had received poor care, and Dr. Hans Hoff, a leading neurosurgeon in Vienna, diagnosed that Nazrul was suffering from Pick's disease
  • 1953 - His condition was judged to be incurable, Nazrul returned to Calcutta.
  • 1962 - Pramila died, and Nazrul remained in intensive medical care. He stopped working due to his deteriorating health.
  • 1972 - The newly independent nation of Bangladesh brought Nazrul to live in Dhaka with the consent of the Government of India.
  • 1976 - He was accorded the citizenship of Bangladesh. Despite receiving treatment and attention, Nazrul's physical and mental health did not improve. Nazrul soon succumbed to his long-standing ailments.In accordance with a wish he had expressed in one of his poems, he was buried beside a mosque on the campus of the University of Dhaka.
